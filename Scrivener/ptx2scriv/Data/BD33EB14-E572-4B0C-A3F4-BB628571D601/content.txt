<p> The discharge capacity, discharge pressure and horsepower required to drive the pump
              varies as shown below with changes in impeller RPM or impeller DIAMETER. </p>
<p> The discharge capacity <m>Q</m> varies DIRECTLY as the impeller speed <m>n</m> or
              impeller diameter <m>d</m>. <me>\left(\frac{Q_2}{Q_1}\right)
                =\left(\frac{n_2}{n_1}\right)=\left(\frac{d_2}{d_1}\right)</me>
</p>
<p> The discharge pressure from the pump varies AS THE SQUARE of the impeller rpm or
              diameter. <me>\left(\frac{p_2}{p_1}\right)
                =\left(\frac{n_2}{n_1}\right)^2=\left(\frac{d_2}{d_1}\right)^2</me>
</p>
<p> The horsepower necessary to drive the pump varies AS THE CUBE of the impeller rpm or
              diameter. <me>\left(\frac{HP_2}{HP_1}\right)
                =\left(\frac{n_2}{n_1}\right)^3=\left(\frac{d_2}{d_1}\right)^3</me>
</p>
