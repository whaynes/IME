Ohm’s law describes the relationship between current, voltage, and resistance. According to Ohm’s Law, the current flowing through a circuit is directly proportional to the voltage across it and inversely proportional to the resistance of the circuit. Mathematically, this relationship is expressed as
\begin{equation*} I = V/R \end{equation*}
where:
  • \(I\) is the the current in amperes
  • \(V\) is the voltage in volts, and
  • \(R\) is the resistance in ohms (Ω).
