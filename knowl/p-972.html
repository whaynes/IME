Low pressure bleed steam is used as the steam supply for the first stage heater. The bleed steam condenses as it gives up its energy to the condensate, and the condensed bleed steam, known as drains is still hot enough to be used to preheat the condensate. The drains then enter the drain cooler section where the remaining heat is recovered, before the drains are returned to the system by vacuum drag to the main condenser.
