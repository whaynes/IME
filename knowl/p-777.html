In engineering a nominal value is a quantity used for the name of an object. Nominal values are usually close to, but not the same as, the actual value. For example a 2\(\times\)4 is the name of a piece of lumber with finished dimensions closer to 1-1/2 inches by 3-1/2 inches. A 120 Volt outlet may provide anywhere between 114 V and 126 V. Nominal values are a convenient shorthand; a rounded figure easy to use and remember.
