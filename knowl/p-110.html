A cross sectional view of a steam atomizer is shown in FigureÂ 1.3.5. In this design, steam under pressure is used to force fuel through the sprayer plate and orifice and into the furnace as small droplets with high velocity which ignite easily and mix thoroughly with the surrounding air. The fuel forms a cone-shaped spray of oil which extends far into the furnace. Steam atomizers are capable of providing good atomization at all steaming rates, however the steam used for atomization is lost from the steam cycle and must be replaced with make-up feed.
