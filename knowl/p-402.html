In DC systems, the power supply maintains a constant polarity, i.e. one terminal is always positive, and the other is always negative, and the supply voltage is usually fairly steady. Batteries and solar cells are examples of DC voltage sources. In DC circuits, current always flows around the circuit in exactly one direction, from the positive to the negative terminal.
