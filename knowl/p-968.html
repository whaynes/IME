A typical air ejector condenser is shown in FigureÂ 7.2.2. The air ejector must operate in two stages to accomplish the necessary increase in pressure to move the air from the main condenser back to the atmosphere. As a result the condenser is also divided into two areas to maintain the pressure differential between the stages. The first condenser is known as the inter-condenser, and it discharges its condensate back to the main condenser. The second stage air ejector steam is condensed in the after-condenser and the condensate collected there is returned to the system by way of the atmospheric drain tank.
