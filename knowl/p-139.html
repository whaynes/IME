Steam pressure and temperature are important parameters to watch while the boiler is operating. Obviously, the purpose of the boiler is to produce steam at the correct pressure and temperature to be used by the main engine and auxiliary machinery. Under normal conditions these values are maintained by the combustion control system and should be relatively steady. A rising or falling steam value is a sure indication of an impending problem. You should carefully observe the normal operating range for the drum and superheater outlet pressures, and notify the engineer or take corrective action if these values deviate from normal.
