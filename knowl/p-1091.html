The main field (4) on the rotor is wound to create eight alternating north and south poles. The main stator core (8) surrounds the main rotor (9) and is wound with three main armature windings (3). As the shaft rotates, the rotor’s magnetic field sweeps by the each of the three armature windings in turn, and induces a sinusoidal voltage in each as shown in Figure 4.4.7. The resulting 3-phase AC is directed to the generator terminals (23), and from there connections are made to the main switchboard.
