The key steps in conducting a job hazard analysis typically include:
  1. Select the job or task to be analyzed.
  2. Break down the job into steps.
  3. Identify potential hazards for each step.
  4. Determine the risk level for each identified hazard. Risk level multiplies severity and the likelihood of each identified hazard.
  5. Develop hazard controls to eliminate or minimize the hazards. This might include engineering controls (e.g., machine guarding), administrative controls (e.g., training or procedures), or personal protective equipment (PPE)
  6. Communicate the findings to workers and stakeholders to ensure awareness and buy-in.
  7. Regularly review and update the job hazard analysis.
