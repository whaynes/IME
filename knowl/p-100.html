The roof, rear, and side waterwall tubes run up the sides and ceiling in the furnace area to protect the furnace walls from too much heat. These tubes normally act as downcomers, carrying water from the steam drum down to the water drum, but at high firing rates may act as generating tubes. Since waterwall tubes absorb the heat of the furnace, less refractory material is required to insulate the boiler, which reduces the total boiler weight.
